I. ASSUMPTIONS AND AIMS OF THE PROPOSED RESEARCH PROJECT
Report Evaluation Score
1st report Scientific objective of the proposed ambitious and modern research proposal is based on the former recognized work carried out by its head. In particular this applies to his description of quantum systems by discrete finite dimensional Hilbert spaces, elaboration of Fourier algorithms for atom states and application of quantum algebras and quantum equations. His design of algorithms for exhaustive generation of codes for quantum error correction is particularly significant. The assumptions, purpose, and goals of the research overlap, are repeated, and are given too generalized definitions so that is difficult to determine a possible influence of this research on a development of other fields. Applications of the research and its fitting into strategic goals are presented in a too generalized way. 4.00
2nd report This is a very ambitious multidisciplinary project engaged into a number of open questions in the field of quantum information. Its head has already carried out a number of very successful projects and many works in the field.

9.1. The main hypothesis is scientifically sound (5) and the field is very vibrant, interesting, important for fundamental research (quantum physics), and a great potential application (computational methods, cryptography). In this field, the distance between basic results and possible application is incredibly short. The project explores what is explored by other similar projects in the World but by means of pretty original own - now partly published - theoretical methods (4.5). Altogether for 9.1 the score is (on average) 4.8.

9.2 Contribution, impact, and research priority alignment are excellent (4.6).

9.3 Application of the proposed research is mostly in further research, and applications in other fields are limited to a future development of quantum computers. In this sense, the project is not self-contained and does not build up a self-contained research with other projects in Croatia. We do not take it amiss since it investigates but a small segment (picked up according to the current available budget and suitability of publishing as many papers as possible) on a road from posing a problem to solving it - a syndrome of the Croatian scientific research scene. Therefore, for this part: for a contribution to possible applications (possibly and regrettably outside of Croatia) the score is 4.5, while for a possibility of using the results in other fields it is 3.5, altogether 3.8. 		4.40
II. RESEARCH PLAN AND METHODS
Report Description Scores
1st Report Project goals are clearly defined but without stating efficient methods and procedures to carry them out, it is not clear whether they are realistically feasible From the enclosed data it is difficult to foresee whether the proposed research timetable could be carried out within the given time window. 4.00
2nd Report 9.2 Research goals are objectively and clearly defined (score 4.8), the plan is feasible (4), and can be realized in time (4). The biggest problem of this project is that all its coworkers are theoreticians and the fourth part of point 9.1 is a pure experiment for which no competence is offered, no collaboration with other projects is indicated, no equipment is either mentioned or asked for, and a realization of the experiment is not mentioned within methods described in section 9.2. It is therefore unclear to me how this part of the project can be realized at all. Because of this, although the rest of the project is excellent and methods are of a high quality, for the part concerning the methodology the partial score is 3.5. Total score of the section 9.2 is (on average) 4.1.

9.5. Taking into account the above comments I would like to add that according to my estimates the other expected results are realistically realizable in time with the team and methods described in 9.5. It is interesting that here the goals promised in 9.4(4) are not mentioned at all, and I am tempted to take the whole 9.4(4) section as a mistake. Thus I am in a moral dilemma whether to consider this part at all and therewith lower the score of otherwise - according to my opinion - flawless project. Total score: 4.4. 		4.25
III. COMPETENCY OF PROJECT HEAD
Report Description Scores

Success of his previous research is reflected in numerous (39) Current Contents paper in quality journal and invited talks (70). He is a permanent referee for Phys. Rev. Lett. and Phys. Rev A. 5.00
2nd Report 10.1. Previous discoveries are well presented and the head of the project has a pioneering role in some of them, hence the score here is clean 5.

10.2. The head has made real world results in his three previous projects, especially in the light of mediocre achievements in Croatia and with respect to the conditions in Croatia. The most important results and ideas achieved so far will be developed further in the present project what shows that the project is well founded on the previous investigations. All the afore mentioned projects are theoretical and the equipment needed by the project boils down to computers that rapidly run out of date, but since the program which the project is part of as well as the the Croatian academic network have considerable resources the project can make use of (SRCE cluster, IRB-GRID cluster, Faculty of Civil Engineering cluster), the lack of the computing equipment within the project itself is not relevant for the project. The bigger problem is that the project lacks any equipment (and a request for one) for carrying out experiments stated in section 9.1(4) of the project. Because of this the score is here 3.9. The total score for this section is 4.4.

The response of the previous research is great and can be best recognized by means of the number of citations, through publishing in highest quality American Physical Society and Elsevier journals, through a Springer invitation to write a book on quantum information and quantum communication, by means of presentation of some results the head of the project obtained in standard textbooks. PROPOSAL TO THE EVALUATION COMMITTEE
Report Proposal
1st Report The head of the project is an internationally recognized specialist for the filed of quantum logic which is the basis for quantum computers assuming the error correction problem would be solved successfully. The research team is small and it is hard to believe it will completely carry out the ambitious research programme proposed in this project. Foreseeable postgraduate students although wished for still do not exist. With respect to the importance of the computer field research in Croatia and in the World, I propose a support of this project to a somewhat changed extent.
Accept with a reduced budget, after evaluating whether that would affect the feasibility of the project.
2nd Report The project is well founded and concentrated on some of the most important question of quantum information and quantum communication. This field has been extensively developed in the last 10 years, and the head of the proposed project has himself taken a pioneering part in some of the results. His connections and cooperation with some of the best scientists in the field and his recognized charisma (Springer invitation to write a book, permanent referee for American Physical Society journals, leader of the Croatian Humboldt-Club) show that he is an exceptionally capable scientist who definitely deserves his own research project.
Accept with the proposed budget.
