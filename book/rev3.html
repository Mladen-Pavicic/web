I would just like to offer an explanation to the reader of this Review.

The reviewer writes: "An example of unprecise use of language is the misleading title of section 1.20. Teleportation does not send a copy of the state but only carries over the state from one atom to another."

The provocative title of Sec. 1.20 ("Teleportation of Copies and the No-Cloning Theorem") is explained in the section (p. 58, bottom) as follows: "We cannot clone unknown qubits, but we can nevertheless copy them. We just have to pay a price for doing so: the original must be destroyed in the process."

I hate to comment on a review and I would not do that for obvious but irrelevant misunderstanding (as e.g., about CMOS) but the one about the "copy" is too grave to be left uncommented. For, if I had really claimed that one could make a copy of an arbitrary quantum state and keep the original state, that would have most likely make the reader brush aside the book.
