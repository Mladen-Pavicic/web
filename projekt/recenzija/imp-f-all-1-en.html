CC is a normed number of Current Contents papers per co-author in the following sense: if an author takes part in writing one paper together with 49 co-authors, two papers with 6 co-authors each and one paper with one co-author, then his/her CC=1/50+2/7+1/2=0.81.
CC measures the productivity of each co-author.

IF is a normed Impact Factor, i.e., an average "impact" per each co-author in the following sense: if an author takes part in writing one paper in a Current Contents journal with Impact Factor 7.489 together with 49 co-authors, two papers with Impact Factor 2.9976 with 6 co-authors each and one paper with Impact Factor 0.632 with one co-author, then his/her IF=7.489/50+2x2.997/7+0.632/2=1.32.
IF measures the "impact" of each co-author.

Ratio IF/CC is characteristic of the subfield an author works in but not for the "popularity" of the author him/herself and/or his results. For this, an indication offers the following citation factor.

CIT is a normed number of citations in Current Contents papers without self-citation and without cross self-citation per each co-author in the following sense: if a an author takes part in writing
- one paper together with 49 co-authors and this paper gets cited 80 times whereof 17 citations are made by co-authors of this paper,
- two papers each with 6 different co-authors and the first paper gets cited only once by one of the co-authors of the second paper and the second paper gets cited only once by one of the co-authors of the first paper and
- one paper with one co-author which gets cited twice by authors who never appear as co-authors of the author,
then CIT=63/50+0/7+0/7+2/2=2.26.
CIT measures the "popularity" of an author and/or his/her results with scientists in the same field around the Globe.
